Executive Summary

The rapid expansion of artificial intelligence is driving an unprecedented build-out of data center infrastructure worldwide. This growth carries significant environmental consequences across multiple dimensions.

Key Projections: By 2026, global data center electricity consumption is projected to reach 1,050 TWh — enough to rank fifth among nations. AI could produce 24-44 million metric tons of CO2 annually by 2030.

5. Impact on Local Communities

5.1 Utility Costs

  • Arizona: 8% rate hike to fund data center electricity
  • Data centers often receive discounted rates
  • Residential customers effectively subsidize Big Tech

5.2 Environmental Justice

The siting of data centers disproportionately affects marginalized communities:

  • Black neighborhoods in Arizona and Tennessee face increased pollution
  • Gas-powered turbines linked to asthma and lung cancer
  • Local water supplies face strain during summer peaks

6. Mitigation Strategies

6.1 Smart Siting

Impact Reduction Potential

Region Suitability Reason
Texas, Montana, Nebraska Optimal Wind resources, lower water stress
Nevada, Arizona Avoid Water scarcity
Northern Virginia Avoid Infrastructure strain
Combined Strategy: Smart siting + grid decarbonization + efficiency could cut CO2 by 73% and water by 86% vs worst-case.

6.2 Cooling Innovations

  • Waterless cooling: Microsoft 2025 design uses zero water
  • Closed-loop systems: Up to 70% freshwater reduction
  • Immersion cooling: Eliminates water entirely

6.3 Energy Efficiency

  • Grid decarbonization: ~15% CO2 reduction by 2030
  • Operational efficiency: Additional 7% CO2, 29% water reduction
  • Nuclear power: Carbon-free baseload (SMRs, existing plants)
