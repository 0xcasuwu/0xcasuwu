Overview

Nootropics are compounds that enhance cognitive function, particularly executive functions, memory, creativity, or motivation. This review examines the evidence base for commonly used nootropic compounds.

Categories Covered: Racetams, Eugeroics (Modafinil), Cholinergics, Adaptogens, and Novel Synthetic Compounds

Nootropic Categories by Evidence Quality

1. Racetams

The racetam family includes piracetam, aniracetam, oxiracetam, and phenylpiracetam. These compounds modulate AMPA receptor activity and enhance cholinergic transmission.

Compound Typical Dose Primary Effects
Piracetam 1200-4800 mg/day Memory, verbal fluency
Aniracetam 750-1500 mg/day Anxiety, creativity
Phenylpiracetam 100-200 mg/day Focus, physical stamina

2. Modafinil & Eugeroics

Modafinil is a wakefulness-promoting agent with demonstrated cognitive enhancement effects in sleep-deprived individuals and modest effects in rested subjects.

Meta-Analysis Finding: Modafinil shows reliable improvements in attention, executive function, and learning in healthy adults, with effect sizes of d = 0.3-0.5.
