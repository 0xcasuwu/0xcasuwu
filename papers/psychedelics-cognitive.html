Overview

Classic psychedelics (psilocybin, LSD, DMT) have emerged as potential cognitive enhancers through their unique ability to promote neuroplasticity and reorganize brain network connectivity.

Key Mechanism: 5-HT2A receptor activation leads to increased dendritic spine density, BDNF release, and enhanced between-network connectivity.

1. Neuroplasticity Effects

Psychedelics are classified as "psychoplastogens" - compounds that rapidly promote lasting neuroplasticity after a single dose.

Neuroplasticity Markers

Effect Magnitude Duration
Dendritic spine density +10% 24h onset, 1+ month
BDNF mRNA expression Elevated Up to 1 month
Synaptogenesis Increased Rapid onset

2. Creativity & Problem-Solving

Psychedelics have been associated with enhanced divergent thinking, novel associations, and creative problem-solving.

Historical Note: Nobel laureate Francis Crick reportedly conceived the DNA double helix structure while under the influence of LSD.

3. Microdosing Research

Microdosing involves taking sub-perceptual doses (typically 1/10th to 1/20th of a full dose) on a regular schedule.

Protocol Dose Schedule
Fadiman Protocol 10-20μg LSD / 0.1-0.3g psilocybin Every 3 days
Stamets Protocol 0.1-0.3g psilocybin + Lion's Mane 4 days on, 3 off
Evidence Status: Controlled trials have produced mixed results, with some studies suggesting placebo effects account for much of the reported benefits.

4. Default Mode Network

Psychedelics decrease activity and connectivity within the DMN while increasing between-network connectivity - a pattern associated with reduced rumination and increased cognitive flexibility.
