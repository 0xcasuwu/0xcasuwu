Abstract

This expanded synthesis integrates findings from systematic reviews, meta-analyses, and pivotal clinical trials examining the efficacy of classic serotonergic psychedelics (psilocybin, LSD, DMT, ayahuasca), MDMA, ibogaine, and ketamine across major depressive disorder, PTSD, substance use disorders, and more.

Key Finding: Psilocybin shows effect sizes of g = -0.66 to -1.49 for depression - approximately 2-4x larger than SSRIs (g ≈ 0.30). MDMA-assisted therapy achieves 67-71% PTSD remission vs. 20-30% for FDA-approved medications.

1. Introduction

1.1 The Burden of Treatment-Resistant Mental Illness

Major depressive disorder affects over 280 million people globally and is the leading cause of disability worldwide. Approximately 30% of individuals with MDD do not respond adequately to two or more trials of antidepressant medication.

1.2 The Research Moratorium (1970-2006)

The ~35-year moratorium on psychedelic research was not precipitated by scientific evidence of harm, but by politically motivated scheduling decisions. The placement of psilocybin and LSD in Schedule I alongside heroin reflects political judgments rather than pharmacological evidence.

Drug Harm Rankings (Nutt et al., 2010)

We knew we couldn't make it illegal to be either against the war or black, but by getting the public to associate the hippies with marijuana and blacks with heroin... we could disrupt those communities."
— John Ehrlichman, Nixon's domestic policy advisor (Baum, 2016)

2. Neurobiological Mechanisms

2.1 5-HT2A Receptor Agonism

Psilocybin is a prodrug of psilocin, a non-selective serotonin receptor agonist with primary affinity for the 5-HT2A receptor. Human studies using ketanserin demonstrate complete blockade of the psychedelic experience, confirming the necessary role of this receptor.

Receptor Binding Profile

2.2 Default Mode Network Modulation

The DMN is implicated in self-referential processing and depressive rumination. Psilocybin significantly decreases blood flow and connectivity within the DMN, while increasing between-network connectivity - a neurobiological effect distinct from SSRIs.

2.3 Neuroplasticity (Psychoplastogens)

Classic psychedelics promote structural neuroplasticity: increased dendritic arbor complexity, spine density, and synaptogenesis - effects mediated through 5-HT2A-dependent TrkB, mTOR, and BDNF signaling.

Davis et al. (2021) - Johns Hopkins: N=27, Cohen's d = 2.5 at week 1. 71% response, 54% remission at week 4.

3.3 Long-Term Follow-Up

12-month follow-up (Gukasyan et al., 2022): 75% response, 58% remission maintained. Median time to depressive event: 189 days (25mg) vs 21 days (1mg control).

4.2 FDA Rejection (2024)

Despite robust data, the FDA voted against MDMA approval citing: functional unblinding (90%+ correct guesses), safety reporting concerns, and inability to parse drug vs. therapy effects.

4.3 Ibogaine for Trauma

Williams et al. (2024) in Nature Medicine: N=30 Special Operations veterans, 100% PTSD response, 86% remission at 1 month.

6. Safety and Adverse Events

6.1 General Safety Profile

Psilocybin LD50 = 280 mg/kg in rats - over 700x the clinical dose. Classic psychedelics are not addictive and show rapid tolerance (tachyphylaxis).

6.2 Systematic Review (Hinkle et al., 2024)

  • 114 studies, 3,504 participants analyzed
  • Serious adverse events rare; none in healthy participants
  • Zero cases of suicide, persistent psychosis, or HPPD in modern clinical research
  • Common nonserious AEs: headache, nausea, transient anxiety
